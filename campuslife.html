Campus Life

Housing

Yale is home to 6,500 Graduate and Professional School students, many of whom prefer to live off campus. The University offers limited on-campus housing options for graduate and PhD students, so the Yale Housing Office maintains a portal with Off-Campus Listings in which the property owners are verified. We recommend starting there, or with listings that will be updated on this page from within the School of Art community, or on the closed Facebook Group. As you look for apartments Yale recommends trying to stay within the Yale Shuttle routes to benefit from this service as well as campus safety resources such as the LiveSafe app, but this is of course up to you.

    • Dining Options

    At Yale, meal plans give you access to 14 residential-college dining halls plus campus-wide retail dining locations — offering flexibility, community meals, and convenience across campus

    Meal Plans
    • Full Plan: Unlimited dining hall meals + retail-dining points (required for first-years, available to all undergraduates).
    • Flex Plan: 14 weekly dining hall swipes + more retail-dining points, great for students with different schedules or living off campus.
    • Connect (Off-Campus) Plan: For students living off campus: limited dining-hall swipes plus retail dining credits.
    • Covers housing, food, utilities, health services, clothing, laundry, travel, and miscellaneous costs
    • Includes Yale Health Plan: ~$3,422 (listed under “Fees”)
    • Students are expected to live modestly and focus primarily on academic work.
